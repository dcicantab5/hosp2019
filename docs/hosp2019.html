Specialist Excesses and Shortages:

Current Sharing General Medicine Workload

Figure 4: Specialist Excesses and Shortages. Negative values are shortages, and positive values are excesses. Calculation of the value for each hospital is by deducting the median standardised workload (of the respective type of facility) from a hospitals current standardised workload. The number in shortage/excess is then derived using that hospitals current workload.

Figure 5: Specialist Excesses and Shortages. Negative values are shortages, and positive values are excesses, relative to the median of the corresponding hospital type. Case studies

Case Study 1 - Drilldown QE

  • Current Sharing workload Fig 4: excess of 18 specialists.
    • Standardised workload based on gen med admissions only, for QE is 3.5 specialists per 1000 admissions (Median for all state hospitals 2.2).
  • All in workload Fig 5: excess of 17 specialists.
    • Standardised workload based on all in admissions, for QE is 3.9 specialists per 1000 admissions (Median for all state hospitals 2.7).
  • Specialty composition: 55% (30) subs, 45% (25) gen med Fig 6.
  • Workload involvement:
    • Gen med admissions handled by GenMed (25), Gastroenterology (7), Geriatrics (1), Haematology (4) and Infectious Disease (1), and Rheumatology (2); (total: 40).
    • Dermatology (3), Nephrology (5), Neurology (2), and Respiratory (5) are not involved in gen med workload (total: 15).
